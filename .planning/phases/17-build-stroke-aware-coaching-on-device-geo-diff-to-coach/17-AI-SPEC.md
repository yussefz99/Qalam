## 1. System Classification

**System Type:** Hybrid — a **stateful, multimodal tutoring agent** (LangGraph on Cloud Run) that (a) *judges* a child's Arabic-letter stroke attempt form- and letter-aware, (b) *explains why* it's wrong, (c) *coaches* in a warm child-appropriate voice, and (d) emits a *renderable ghost-correction* — over a reused deterministic-scorer floor and ML Kit recognition body.

**Description:**
Qalam's tutor watches a child (ages 5–10, heritage learner) handwrite Arabic letters stroke-by-stroke on a tablet and responds like a patient human teacher. The redesign turns today's **baa-isolated-only** tutor into one that is **form-aware** (isolated/initial/medial/final), **generalizes from curriculum data** to all 28 letters × 4 forms with no per-letter hardcoding, **explains the specific fix** (not "wrong"), **remembers each child** (a per-child learner model of strengths/struggles), and **shows the fix** as a ghost stroke over the child's own attempt. "Good" = a grounded verdict a real Arabic teacher would agree with, plus warm, specific, English-primary coaching in the owner's mother's voice, delivered within the presence budget.

**Critical Failure Modes:** (the behaviors that absolutely cannot go wrong)
1. **Ungrounded verdict** — praising or advancing a child on a *failed* attempt, or contradicting the correctness truth (GROUND-03 zero-tolerance).
2. **Form-blindness** — accepting the wrong positional shape as correct (today's F5: an isolated baa passed as a correct medial/final).
3. **Off-voice coaching** — full-Arabic (F3) or generic "that's wrong" (F4) feedback; anything that isn't the warm, specific, English-primary mother's register.
4. **Cold-start strict-fail** — the first attempt after idle falling back to the strict scorer and false-failing correct writing (today's F2).
5. **Child-data leakage** — storing raw strokes/PII in the per-child learner model; the model must hold only derived, minimal, parent-controlled patterns.

## 1b. Domain Context

> Researched by `gsd-domain-researcher` (2026-07-01).

**Industry Vertical:** Children's education technology — early Arabic literacy / handwriting instruction (heritage learners)
**User Population:** Children aged 5–10 who hear Arabic at home but cannot yet read or write it, practicing on an Android/iPad tablet with a stylus. The evaluation authority is the owner's mother, a graduate-degreed Arabic teacher.
**Stakes Level:** **High** (pedagogical correctness is the entire value proposition) + a **Critical** child-data-privacy dimension. A tutor that passes a wrong letter, teaches a wrong stroke, or discourages a child does real harm to a young learner's foundation and destroys the trust that separates Qalam from a $60/hr human tutor.
**Output Consequence:** The verdict advances/holds a child and awards/withholds a mastery star; the coaching line is the child's entire correction. A false pass installs a wrong motor habit that must later be un-learned; a false fail teaches a child their correct work is wrong and erodes confidence; off-register/full-Arabic coaching is simply not understood by a heritage learner and the lesson is lost.

### What Domain Experts Evaluate Against

An expert does **not** judge a single "accuracy" score — she judges shape, direction, stroke order, position vs. reference lines, and kinematics, *plus* the Arabic dot/connection rules. The curriculum data already encodes this vocabulary (boat/bowl, tooth, tail, hump, dot-below, stroke count/order, `rightToLeft`/`topToBottom`) — which is what makes it generalizable per G2/G5.

| Dimension | Good (expert accepts) | Bad (expert flags) | Stakes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Positional-form correctness** | Attempt matches the shape *for the position asked* — isolated baa = full bowl+dot; medial = small tooth joined both sides; final = bowl+tail | Isolated bowl accepted when medial tooth was requested (form-blind) | **Critical** (UAT F5) |
| **Dot identity (i'jam): count + above/below** | Correct number, correct side — baa = one dot **below**, even medial/final | Dot missing / wrong count / wrong side, silently accepted (a dot *above* baa is a different letter) | **Critical** |
| **Stroke order, direction, count** | Body first (RTL/top-to-bottom), dots last, expected stroke count — watch the hand, not just the picture | Dot-first, left-to-right body, collapsed strokes — accepted because the final image looks close | **High** |
| **Shape fidelity within child tolerance** | Bowl deep enough to read as a bowl; alif one straight tall line; normal 5–10-yr wobble accepted | *Either*: a flat "line" baa passed as a bowl; *or* a correct shaky child bowl **rejected** by a strict threshold | **High** (UAT F2) |
| **Coaching that names the fix + explains WHY** | "Your bowl is too flat, so your baa looks like a line — try again, slower." Specific, states flaw + consequence, English + a sprinkle of Arabic | "Oops, try again!" / "That's wrong." — generic, binary, or full-Arabic to a child who can't read it | **High** (UAT F3/F4/F6) |

### Known Failure Modes in This Domain

1. **Form-blindness** — validates "is it a baa?" not "is it the *medial* baa asked for" (UAT F5, P0). Good avoids it via curriculum-driven form-awareness (G2).
2. **False-fail of correct writing** — a clean shaky child baa rejected by a strict threshold or a cold-start fallback to the strict scorer (UAT F2, P0). The single most damaging failure for confidence. Scorer kept as offline floor only (G3/G5).
3. **Generic/binary feedback & over-praise** — no diagnosis, or correct/wrong with no coaching, or praise-on-fail advancing sloppy work (UAT F4/F6; anti-over-praise in G3).
4. **Off-register / wrong-language coaching** — full Arabic or chatbot-cheerful to a 5–10-yr-old heritage learner (UAT F3; G4).
5. **Penalizing normal childhood motor variation** — treating age-typical wobble/size/baseline drift as error (distinct from #2).

### Regulatory / Compliance Context

Population is children <13 → **COPPA** governs (GDPR-K / Age-Appropriate-Design analogues for EU/UK). The **amended COPPA Rule** (effective 2025-06-23; most obligations due 2026-04-22) expands "personal information" to explicitly include **biometric, persistent-identifier, geolocation, and behavioral/inferred data**, and requires **data minimization** + **verifiable parental consent before collection**.

- **The per-child learner model (G8) is regulated data.** `struggles[]`/`strengths[]` are *inferred/behavioral* data. Storing **derived patterns** ("shaky final forms," "drops dots") is defensible under minimization; storing **raw strokes, writing images, or PII** is not — avoid or gate behind explicit consent. Private-by-default, parent-controlled.
- **Consent debt to close before launch:** the AI-owns-verdict + "send the rendered attempt image off-device" reversals (GROUND-01/02) need a proper **ADR + consent copy** — sending a child's writing image off-device is a data flow consent must cover. Parked ideas (family voice recordings, writing-image portfolio) touch **biometric/voiceprint** categories — explicit consent + minimization first.
- Children never log in (parent-gated, PIN); child-facing surface collects the minimum.

### Domain Expert Roles for Evaluation

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| **Owner's mother — graduate Arabic teacher (primary authority)** | Labels the **gold set** (per letter × form: pass/acceptable/fail + why); signs off per-form correctness (`signedOff`); authors + approves **mother's-voice** coaching lines; calibrates the pass/acceptable/fail boundary for real 5–10-yr motor variation; reviews G7 "why" + G9 corrections for pedagogical truth. Nothing model-authored ships unsigned. |
| **Owner (product + pedagogy proxy)** | Adjudicates register/voice, tolerance presets, curriculum breadth; owns anti-gamification + child-data constraints; escalation when the mother is unavailable. |
| **LLM judge (calibrated, not trusted blind)** | Runs form-aware pass/fail + coaching at scale; calibrated to the mother's gold labels (target ≥0.7 agreement) before trusted; every advance/star is grounding-guarded (G3). |
| **Parent (production signal, not labeler)** | Consent authority for the learner model + any image/voice storage; the dashboard/"teacher's note" consumes derived data, not eval labels. |

## 2. Framework Decision

**Selected Framework:** **LangGraph** (Python) on Cloud Run + **`langchain-google-vertexai`** (Gemini 2.5-flash on Vertex AI). **Reuse of the existing stack — confirmed, not re-selected** (owner reuse-boundary lock, 2026-07-01).

**Version:** as currently pinned in `server/pyproject.toml` / the deployed `qalam-tutor` revision (LangGraph + langchain-google-vertexai already in production; pin-bump only if a researched pattern requires it).

**Rationale:**
- The tutor is a **stateful, multi-step, grounded** agent with hard safety invariants (never praise/advance-on-fail) and an audit/eval gate — LangGraph's checkpointed graph + guard nodes is the documented fit for "regulated / audit-trail / human-in-the-loop" stateful agents, and it is **already built, deployed, and validated** (Phases 14–16). Rebuilding the framework is pure risk against the reuse boundary.
- **Keyless Gemini on Vertex** is already live (Technion credits, ADC/runtime SA — no API key on device or server). Multimodal (image) judging is native to Gemini 2.5-flash.
- The **Claude-on-Vertex** coach remains a future drop-in via env swap (the coach node returns unbound), so the model choice for the *coaching/voice* leg is not locked in by this decision.

**Alternatives Considered:**

| Framework / shape | Ruled Out Because |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Single multimodal call (no graph)** | Genuinely considered for the judge+coach path (D6) — lower latency/cost, simpler. Kept **on the table as an internal node-collapse** *within* LangGraph, not as a framework swap: we keep the graph's grounding guards + streaming + checkpointing while optionally collapsing judge+coach+correction into one Gemini call for the write/trace path. Decided per D6 in research. |
| Rebuild on Google ADK / CrewAI / direct SDK | Throws away the validated LangGraph spine + client seam + grounding harness for no capability gain; violates the reuse boundary. |

**Vendor Lock-In Accepted:** Partial — Vertex/Gemini for inference (mitigated: keyless ADC, and the coach leg is env-swappable to Claude-on-Vertex). No new lock-in beyond what's already in production.

## 3. Framework Quick Reference — LangGraph + langchain-google-vertexai (keyless Gemini on Vertex)

> Fetched from official docs by `gsd-ai-researcher` (2026-07-01). Stack LOCKED — reuse the deployed `qalam-tutor` Cloud Run service (LangGraph + FastAPI + keyless Gemini 2.5-flash on Vertex via `langchain-google-vertexai`, project `qalam-app-bd7d0`, `us-central1`, ADC — no API key). Claude-on-Vertex is a future env-swap via `ChatAnthropicVertex` (already wired in `app/models.py`).

**Installed (matches `server/pyproject.toml`, python ≥3.12):** `langgraph>=1.2,<2` · `langchain>=1.0,<2` (`init_chat_model`) · `langchain-google-vertexai>=3.2.4` (`ChatVertexAI` + `ChatAnthropicVertex`) · `fastapi>=0.115` · `pydantic>=2.9,<3`. No key — Cloud Run runtime SA supplies ADC; locally `gcloud auth application-default login`.

**Entry-point — a multimodal STRUCTURED verdict (upgrades `image_judge.judge_baa_image` from regex JSON to Pydantic):**
```python
from langchain_google_vertexai import ChatVertexAI
from langchain_core.messages import HumanMessage, SystemMessage
from pydantic import BaseModel, ConfigDict, Field

class Verdict(BaseModel):
    model_config = ConfigDict(extra="forbid")
    verdict: str = Field(description='"pass" or "needsWork"')
    whyExplanation: str = Field(description="one child-facing diagnosis")
    coachingLine: str = Field(description="one warm, specific line naming the ONE fix")

def build_judge() -> ChatVertexAI:
    # thinking_budget=0 is LOAD-BEARING: Gemini 2.5-flash defaults thinking ON, which
    # slows the call AND was observed to return an EMPTY structured reply. 0 disables it.
    return ChatVertexAI(model="gemini-2.5-flash", project="qalam-app-bd7d0",
                        location="us-central1", temperature=0.0, max_tokens=400, thinking_budget=0)

def judge(system_prompt: str, image_b64: str) -> Verdict:
    model = build_judge().with_structured_output(Verdict)   # returns a validated Verdict instance
    msg = HumanMessage(content=[
        {"type": "text", "text": "Grade this child's attempt."},
        {"type": "image_url", "image_url": {"url": f"data:image/png;base64,{image_b64}"}}])
    return model.invoke([SystemMessage(content=system_prompt), msg])
```
- The `data:image/png;base64,...` data-URL form is what `image_judge.py` already sends successfully — keep it (don't churn to the newer content-block form).
- `with_structured_output(Verdict)` returns a **validated instance** (Gemini tool-calling under the hood; re-prompts on schema violation, raises on hard failure → the caller's `needsWork` fail-safe still applies).

**Key abstractions (in this system):** `StateGraph`/`TutorState` (`app/graph.py`) · node = `state→partial state`, each binds its own model via `init_chat_model` (`app/models.py`) · `bind_tools(..., tool_choice="any")` = the closed 4-tool ACTION lock (`coach.py`/`tools.py`) · `with_structured_output(Model)` = the redesigned judge · `InMemorySaver` = per-process, keeps the server stateless (no child data persists across requests).

**Pitfalls (learned in-repo):**
1. **Gemini thinking breaks structured output** — always `thinking_budget=0` for Vertex Gemini (`_provider_kwargs` in `models.py`).
2. **Building the model per-request is the F2 cold-start bug** — `image_judge._build_judge()` runs on every call → first-after-idle >12s. Fix = singleton/`@lru_cache` + FastAPI `lifespan` warm-up (§4).
3. **`extra="forbid"` is a two-sided 422 trap** — `TutorFactsIn` (server) and Dart `TutorFacts.toJson()` must mirror field-for-field; ship the server field FIRST. This is the GROUND-02 non-PII guard.
4. **`/healthz` never reaches the container** (Google edge reserves it) — the warm-up route is `/health`.
5. **Claude-on-Vertex is a different region+class** — `ChatAnthropicVertex` at `location="global"` (not `us-central1`); env swap (`COACH_MODEL_PROVIDER=anthropic_vertex`), gated on a human enabling the Model-Garden card.

**Folder (reuse, don't reinvent):** `server/app/{main.py (/health,/coach, wait_for budget, image short-circuit), graph.py, models.py, image_judge.py, schema.py, prompts.py, nodes/, curriculum.py+curriculum_data/}`.

**Sources:** LangChain ChatVertexAI + structured-output + multimodal-messages docs; FastAPI lifespan; Gemini 2.5 thinking docs; in-repo `image_judge.py`/`models.py`/`main.py`/`schema.py`/`pyproject.toml`.

| (gated) coach upgrade | claude-haiku-4-5@… | anthropic_vertex | global | 0.5 | 256 | `ChatAnthropicVertex`, env-swap only |

`max_tokens` is **always** set. Confirm exact model-id strings at deploy (they shift release-to-release; env-overridable via `COACH_MODEL`/`COACH_MODEL_PROVIDER`).

**D5 — ONE data-driven, FORM- + LETTER-aware prompt (no per-letter hardcoding).** `contextualForms` in `letters.json` already holds, per form, a `commonMistakes` list `{id, check, feedback}` + `referenceStrokes`. Inject it into ONE prompt keyed by `letterId` + `targetForm` — this is how 28 letters × 4 forms scale with zero per-letter prompts:
```python
# server/app/rubric.py
def build_rubric(letters_json, letter_id, form):
    letter = next(l for l in letters_json["letters"] if l["id"] == letter_id)
    cf = letter["contextualForms"][form]                 # isolated|initial|medial|final
    mistakes = "\n".join(f"- {m['id']}: {m['feedback']}" for m in cf["commonMistakes"])
    return (f"LETTER: {letter['char']} ({letter['name']}), FORM: {form}.\n"
            f"WHAT CORRECT LOOKS LIKE / COMMON MISTAKES (authored, signed off):\n{mistakes}")
```
Persona + grounding rules stay cache-stable in the `SystemMessage`; the per-attempt `{rubric}` is data in the `HumanMessage`. The `commonMistakes[*].feedback` are the **mother-authored** register (only expose `signedOff:true` forms) — grounds coaching in her voice + curriculum (G4). The closed action-space + verdict lock still prevent inventing letters/exercises.

**D6 — Agent shape: collapse the judge+coach for the image path, KEEP the graph.**
- **Single multimodal judge+coach+correction call** (`{verdict, whyExplanation, coachingLine, correction}`) — **wins for the trace/write image path:** one round-trip (presence budget), lowest cost, and the model that *sees* the image is the one that coaches it (best "why your bowl is flat" grounding). Replaces the two-hop scorer→coach.
- **The analyze→plan→coach LangGraph** wins for **cross-session personalization + next-exercise selection** (G8/D8): analyze extracts struggle/strength structure, plan walks the curriculum rail, coach speaks — not image-bound, benefits from the checkpointed graph.
- **Decision:** keep the LangGraph structure; make the image judge a **node inside the graph** (`judge` node with `with_structured_output`) that, when `strokeImage` is present, emits verdict + coaching in one call, then routes to a thin `plan` for selection — bypassing the scorer-bound `coach`. Promote `main.py`'s `if facts_in.strokeImage:` short-circuit into a graph entry edge. Preserves the grounding guards (G3 verdict lock, G4 curriculum membership) while removing a hot-path round-trip.

**D6/F2 — Cold-start fix: lifespan warm-up + singleton client:**
```python
# server/app/main.py
from contextlib import asynccontextmanager
from functools import lru_cache

@lru_cache(maxsize=1)
def get_judge():
    from app.image_judge import build_judge
    return build_judge()                       # build ONCE per process, not per request

@asynccontextmanager
async def lifespan(app):
    judge = get_judge()
    try:                                        # prime the Vertex channel/auth so the first REAL call is ~1s
        await asyncio.to_thread(judge.invoke, [HumanMessage(content="ok")])
    except Exception:
        pass                                    # warm-up is best-effort; never block startup
    yield

app = FastAPI(title="Qalam Tutor Server", lifespan=lifespan)
```
`@lru_cache` guarantees the singleton even if warm-up is skipped. The client `/health` ping masks the *container* cold-start; the lifespan warm-up masks the *model-client* cold-start — together they cover both. `min-instances=0` saves idle cost but reintroduces the container cold path (covered by /health + lifespan); `min-instances=1` removes it entirely (a cost/latency tradeoff for the owner). Per-request budget stays `asyncio.wait_for(..., 8s)` → 503 → AuthoredFallback floor.

**D9 — Renderable "ghost correction" output (split the concern):** prefer **on-device geometry** for the correction path — repurpose `.planning/spikes/_lib/geometry.py` (`geometric_diff`/`resample_strokes`/`deepen`), which already emits normalized-0..1 polylines. The corrected polyline is a real interpolation toward the authored `referenceStrokes`, so the ghost is *always anatomically correct* and raw points never leave the device (honors GROUND-02/04). The **model owns `strokeIndex` + `whyExplanation`** (semantic, it sees the image); the **device owns `correctedPolyline`** (geometric).
```python
class GhostCorrection(BaseModel):
    model_config = ConfigDict(extra="forbid")
    strokeIndex: int = Field(description="which authored stroke (0=body, 1=dot)")
    issue: str = Field(description="semantic tag, e.g. 'bowl too shallow' | 'dot above'")
    correctedPolyline: list[list[float]] = Field(default_factory=list,
        description="normalized 0..1 [[x,y],...] animated over the child's stroke; y down; computed on-device")
```

## 4b. AI Systems Best Practices

**Structured outputs with Pydantic.** Define verdict + correction as Pydantic (`extra="forbid"` rejects hallucinated fields). `model.with_structured_output(TutorVerdict)` on `ChatVertexAI` → validated instance (Gemini tool-calling; re-prompts on schema error). For the graph's `coach` node, structured *action* selection is `bind_tools(ACTION_TOOLS, tool_choice="any")` (the closed 4-tool lock). **Retries:** cap 1–2 (child waiting; 8s budget). Log raw reply + `ValidationError`. **Never surface a parse failure to the child**; on retries-exhausted, **fail closed to `needsWork`** (a glitch must never award a star) + the authored floor line — mirrors `image_judge`'s fail-safe and `main.py`'s `StructuredOutputError → 503 → AuthoredFallback`.

**Async-first.** FastAPI routes `async def`; graph via `await _graph().ainvoke(...)`. Vertex `.invoke` is blocking → wrap `await asyncio.to_thread(judge.invoke, ...)` (as `main.py` already does). **The one mistake:** `asyncio.run()` inside the running loop → `RuntimeError`/blocks everything. **Await, don't stream** the verdict — you need the complete structured object to validate before the child sees anything (a half-streamed verdict can't be schema-checked or trusted to award a star). Streaming is a later TTS-only nicety.

**Prompt discipline.** Stable persona + grounding + action rules in the `SystemMessage` (cache-eligible, byte-stable → Vertex prompt caching); variable FACTS/rubric/image in the `HumanMessage` — **never concatenate FACTS into the system prompt**. Few-shot: mother's-voice exemplars are *register to match, never lines to copy* (the `COACH_STROKE_ADDENDUM` fix stops verbatim parroting); the per-form mistake taxonomy is dynamically retrieved from `contextualForms` (D5). Register (G4): English-primary, short sentences, one concrete fix, a *sprinkle* of Arabic only when it fits — never full-Arabic, never chatbot-cheerful. `max_tokens` always set (256 coach / 400 verdict+correction).

**Context management.** This is a tutoring system, not RAG — the window isn't the constraint; **latency + cost per call** are. Cap what `analyze` sees to the **last N attempts** (≈8) + the derived `struggleTags`/`strengthTags` summary (the learner-model summary *is* the compaction), not raw full history. Cross-session (G8): load the recompiled per-child `struggles[]`/`strengths[]` (derived, minimal, private-by-default), never raw strokes.

**Cost & latency.** One Gemini 2.5-flash multimodal invoke ≈ a small rendered PNG (~256–512px, frame-filling) + ~300 tok in / ~150 out = a fraction of a cent/attempt — comfortable for a course-project budget (confirm per-1M pricing at deploy). Latency budget ~1s warm (presence G5); warm-up + `thinking_budget=0` buy the warm number; 8s `wait_for` is the hard ceiling → 503 → floor. **Caching:** byte-stable `SystemMessage` → Vertex prompt caching; add an exact-match cache on `(letterId, targetForm, imageHash)` to skip re-judging identical re-submits (no semantic cache needed — inputs are images + small structured facts). The env-driven routing table lets the eval bake-off route `analyze`/`plan` to the cheapest capable model and reserve a stronger model (gated Claude-on-Vertex) for the *voice* — no code change.

_Verify at build time (flagged, not guessed): exact Gemini/Claude model-id strings (env-overridable) and current Vertex per-1M-token pricing for the cost line._

## 5. Evaluation Strategy

> Designed by `gsd-eval-planner` (2026-07-01). **What changed vs 14-AI-SPEC §5:** that section
> evaluated a tutor where the *scorer owned pass/fail* (GROUND-01) and the agent owned only words.
> The redesign reverses this — **the AI judge now owns the verdict** (`image_judge.py`, reverses
> GROUND-01/02) — so eval is no longer just "is the coaching faithful to a frozen verdict" but
> **"is the verdict itself correct, form-aware, and grounded, AND does the coaching/correction stay
> faithful to it."** The scorer is demoted to an **offline floor**, so a new dimension —
> **form-correctness accuracy** — enters the gate. This section is the regression **gate** (`make eval`)
> that turns today's F2–F6 into permanent, failing-if-reintroduced tests. Principle (ai-evals.md):
> code checks first, LLM judges only for subjective quality, the owner's mother for calibration +
> form ground-truth. Grounding is **zero-tolerance**.

### Dimensions

| # | Dimension (→ goal / bug) | Rubric (PASS / FAIL) | Measurement | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **E1** | **Grounding faithfulness** (G3; F-note praise-drift) | PASS: on `needsWork` the line treats it as not-mastered (correction/"almost"/"try again"), asserts no false geometry, never praises/advances/stars. FAIL: any praise/advance/star-on-fail, or a line asserting geometry the attempt doesn't support. | **Code** (zero-tolerance praise-lexicon + `advance`⇒`pass` hard check, extends `faithfulness.py`) **+ LLM judge** (semantic verdict-agreement — catches paraphrased praise) | **Critical — zero-tolerance** |
| **E2** | **Do-not-assert-false-geometry** (G7; grounding sub-check) | PASS: every geometric claim is true of *this* attempt. FAIL: names a defect/feature not present (invents a tail, a "flat bowl" on a deep one). | **LLM judge** (attempt+line → each claim supported?) **+ Human** (mother spot-check) | **Critical** |
| **E3** | **Form-correctness accuracy per positional form** (G2; **gates F5**) | PASS: judge accepts a shape **iff** it is the correct *positional* form asked. An isolated bowl for a medial/final slot must FAIL. | **Code** (confusion-matrix over a form-labeled set; the `isolated→medial/final` off-diagonal must be **0**) **+ Human** (mother labels per-form ground-truth) | **Critical — gate on F5** |
| **E4** | **Coaching register / voice** (G4; **gates F3, F4**) | PASS: 1–2 short warm sentences a 5–10-yr-old understands, **English** + at most a sprinkle of well-formed Arabic, names the concrete fix. FAIL: full-Arabic (F3), generic "that's wrong" (F4), chatbot-cheer, wall of text, malformed Arabic. | **LLM judge** (`gemini-2.5-flash`, judge≠coach, ≥0.7 vs mother) **+ Code** (non-Latin-char ratio ceiling ⇒ full-Arabic FAIL; length) **+ Human** (mother signs rubric) | **Critical — gate on F3/F4** |
| **E5** | **Explain-why quality** (G7) | PASS: diagnoses the *why* tied to the real defect, specific side/place/feature; distinct verdicts → distinct lines. FAIL: template/boilerplate reused verbatim, or generic "make it neater". | **LLM judge** (specificity + matches-real-defect) **+ Code** (verbatim-exemplar/duplicate-line detector) **+ Human** | **High** |

FAIL: schema-invalid, contradicts the verdict, or points at the wrong feature. | **Code** (schema/bounds + target-form==asked-form) **+ LLM judge** (matches E5 fix + E1 verdict) **+ Human** | **High** |
| **E7** | **Latency/cost + reliability** (G5; **gates F2**) | PASS: warm p95 within the presence budget; **cold-start does NOT drop to the strict scorer** (model pre-warmed) — first post-idle `/coach` returns a real AI verdict. | **E8** | **Graceful degradation (offline floor)** (G5) | PASS: on a real outage the client renders the mother-signed `AuthoredFallback` within budget; loop never blocks; no raw error to the child. FAIL: hang, raw error, blocked exercise. | **Code** (fault-injection: force timeout/5xx/malformed → assert fallback within budget) | **High** |

## 6. Guardrails

Two catastrophic behaviors run **online, every request** (grounding + language); quality signals run **offline** as the flywheel. Guardrails are cheap deterministic checks — negligible latency.

### Online (Real-Time — every request)

| Guardrail | Trigger | Intervention |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| **OG1 — Grounding: block praise/advance/star-on-fail** (E1) | Verdict `needsWork` but coaching has a praise token, OR an advance/star action on a `needsWork`. | **Block** the contradiction → grounded `say`/AuthoredFallback; **never** star a fail. Zero-tolerance (extends `faithfulness.py` from offline report to runtime assert). |
| **OG2 — Language guard: block full-Arabic** (E4 / **F3**) | Coaching line's non-Latin ratio exceeds the ceiling. | **Reject** → re-ask for English, or fall to the English AuthoredFallback. A sprinkle of Arabic passes; a full-Arabic sentence is blocked. |
| **OG3 — Do-not-assert-false-geometry** (E2) | Coaching/correction asserts a feature not in the judge's findings. | **Strip/re-ask**; on repeat → AuthoredFallback. |
| **OG4 — Form-aware verdict lock** (E3 / **F5**) | Judge would accept a shape ≠ the asked positional form. | Judge prompt is **form-parameterized** (structurally can't accept cross-form); dispatcher also rejects a pass whose detected-form ≠ asked-form → `needsWork` + form-specific fix. |
| **OG5 — Ghost-correction schema + consistency** (E6) | Correction path schema-invalid, off-canvas, or target-form ≠ asked / contradicts verdict. | **Drop** the correction (still show verdict + line); log to flywheel. A malformed teaching gesture never renders. |
| **OG6 — Degradation floor** (E7/E8 / **F2**) | Per-request timeout, retry-exhausted, any 5xx. | **Degrade** to the mother-signed AuthoredFallback within budget. **Note F2:** a *cold-start* 503 must be **prevented** (keep-warm), not just caught — falling to the strict scorer on a correct letter is itself a failure, not graceful degradation. |

### Offline (Flywheel — sampled batch)

| Metric | Sampling | Action on degradation |
|--------|----------|----------------------|
| **Register drift (E4)** + Arabic correctness | Calibrated judge over sampled lines; weight to retries/longest/borderline | Below mother's threshold → owner + bilingual review; re-tune coach / re-run Claude-vs-Gemini bake-off; add exemplars |
| **Form-accuracy trend (E3)** per form | Confusion matrix over sampled judged attempts | Drift in the `isolated→medial/final` cell (F5) or per-form accuracy drop → mother re-labels; tighten form prompt |
| **Explain-why specificity (E5)** | Judge over sampled `needsWork` + duplicate detector | Rising template/duplicate rate → coach parroting; re-tune addendum; expand gold |
| **Ghost-correction on-target (E6)** | Judge + human spot-check | Corrections drifting off the flagged defect → revisit D9 geometry→correction mapping |
| **Cold-start / 503 rate (E7 / F2)** | Code metric from traces | Rising → keep-warm/min-instances regression; investigate before demo |
| **Grounding PASS-rate (E1)** | Code check over full batch (target 100%) | Any offline miss that escaped OG1 → release-blocker; add exact case to CI |
| **Cost / latency per moment** | Code metric from traces | Above budget → keep-warm, collapse judge+coach, or cheaper model |

## 7. Production Monitoring

**Tracing tool:** none wired in `server/` today — **wire one before the demo.** **Langfuse** (self-hosted OSS) recommended (LangGraph-compatible; self-hosting keeps child-derived traces off a commercial SaaS under COPPA); **Arize Phoenix** / **LangSmith** alternatives. One trace per `/coach` with per-node spans (judge → coach → correction) carrying model id, tokens, latency, cost, structured verdict/findings, coaching line, correction path. COPPA/D-10: non-PII FACTS only, no-training-use, retention/deletion policy.

**Key metrics:** (1) **Grounding PASS-rate (E1)** target 100% — any contradiction is sev-1; (2) **503/cold-start-fallback rate (E7/F2)** — the P0 demo risk, target ~0% with keep-warm; (3) **verdict-vs-scorer disagreement (E3)** per form — a spike in the isolated↔medial/final cell is the F5 signal; (4) **coaching-language violations (E4/F3)** — the product-signature health metric; (5) **AuthoredFallback rate (E8)**; (6) **ghost-correction drop rate (E6)**; (7) **cost & latency per moment**.

**Alert thresholds:** **Page (sev-1):** E1 < 100%; any star/advance on a `needsWork`; any full-Arabic line reaching a child. **Alert (sev-2):** cold-start/503-fallback > 0 sustained (F2); F5 form-confusion reappears; AuthoredFallback > 10%; p95 over budget. **Review (sev-3):** E4/E5 drift below the mother's threshold; correction drop-rate climbing; cost/session over budget.

**Smart sampling** (every line reaches a child unmediated): all borderline `needsWork` (E1/E2); **all cross-form cases** (F5 blast radius); every first-call-after-idle (F2); OG2-flagged lines + dropped corrections; repeated-struggle sessions (≥3 fails on the same stroke); plus a **random baseline** to catch signal-metric divergence (e.g. abandonment while metrics stay green) → route to the mother for fresh labels, grow `gold_set.jsonl`.
