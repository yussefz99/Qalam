<objective>
Close Fix B from 06-FIXES.md: dots are invisible in both the "Watch me write" stroke-order
animation AND the dotted trace guide for 15 letters (baa, taa, thaa, jeem, khaa, dhaal, zaay,
sheen, daad, zhaa, ghayn, faa, qaaf, noon, yaa). Two compounding causes: (1)
`ReferencePath.resolve()` discards the `type` field, so painters can't tell a `dot` from a
1-point line; and (2) a single-point dot becomes a `moveTo`-only zero-length subpath that
`computeMetrics()` never draws. Fix: render `type == "dot"` strokes as a calm filled ink
circle in both painters, keep dots out of the polyline length math, and advance the animation
a small fixed beat per dot.

Purpose: A child watching or tracing a dotted letter must see where the dot goes — without
it, baa/taa/thaa and 12 others are visually wrong, breaking the trace loop (S1-01, S1-09).
Output: Updated `reference_path.dart`, `stroke_order_animation.dart`, `stroke_canvas.dart`,
and extended animation test proving a dot stroke paints a circle.

Anti-gamification (CLAUDE.md): the dot is a calm filled ink-color circle. The gold reward
color stays reward-exclusive (the start-dot and pen-tip remain gold; the dot itself is ink,
not gold). No bounce, no hype.
</objective>
