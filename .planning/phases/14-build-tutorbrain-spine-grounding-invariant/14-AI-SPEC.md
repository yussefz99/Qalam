## 1. System Classification

**System Type:** Autonomous Agent (single, stateful tutoring agent: analyze → plan → coach over a closed 4-tool action space; not RAG, not a multi-agent crew)

**Description:**
A server-side tutoring agent for Qalam (Android Flutter app teaching children 5–10 to handwrite Arabic). After the on-device deterministic scorer judges a traced letter, the agent receives derived, non-PII FACTS (the scored-attempt trajectory + a session learner model), **analyzes** the child's struggle pattern, **plans** a short next-exercise sequence over baa's authored configs, and emits a warm, specific, Arabic-register **coaching** line — acting only through 4 ACTION tools (`present_activity`, `say`, `give_hint`, `advance`). "Good" = the coaching never contradicts the geometry, names the exact fix, sounds like the owner's-mother teacher voice for a 5–10-year-old, and the plan visibly responds to recent mistakes. The deterministic scorer always owns pass/fail + the mastery star; the agent owns the words and the sequence.

**Critical Failure Modes:**
<!-- The 3-5 behaviors that absolutely cannot go wrong in this system -->
1. **Contradicting / overriding the verdict** — praising a failed stroke, claiming mastery the scorer never granted, or any path that flips a fail to a pass (the scorer owns the verdict; agent reads it as a frozen FACT).
2. **Leaking child data** — raw stroke coordinates or any PII (nickname/name) reaching the network payload; only derived non-PII facts may cross.
3. **Dead-ending the child** — server/model timeout or error that blocks the trace loop instead of degrading to the AuthoredFallback floor.
4. **Emitting an action outside the closed 4-tool set**, or fabricating curriculum/exercises that aren't authored, signed-off baa configs.
5. **Wrong-register or incorrect Arabic** coaching (cold/generic "try again", or malformed Arabic) — the tutor's voice is the product's signature.

## 1b. Domain Context

> Researched by `gsd-domain-researcher`. Grounds the evaluation strategy in domain expert knowledge.

**Industry Vertical:** Children's literacy / handwriting education — specifically **Arabic handwriting instruction for heritage learners** (children who hear Arabic spoken at home but cannot yet read or write it). This is early-literacy / penmanship pedagogy, not foreign-language vocabulary drilling — the closest practitioner analogue is a one-on-one Kumon-style writing tutor or a weekend-school Arabic teacher, not a language app.

**User Population:** Children aged **5–10**, tracing letters on an Android tablet with a stylus, in a parent-supervised, daily-practice ("a little every day") setting. They are pre-literate in Arabic and emotionally fragile about getting it "wrong" — a wrong word from the tutor lands hard. A parent sits nearby or reviews progress; the parent is a secondary stakeholder who must trust the tutor's judgment. The true domain expert who defines "correct" is the **owner's mother — a graduate-degreed Arabic teacher** — whose stroke order, intro order, clean-reps-to-advance, and per-letter common-mistakes spec is the ground truth the agent coaches against.

**Stakes Level:** **High.** Not safety-of-life, but the stakes are a child's *early relationship with handwriting and with the Arabic language itself*. The dominant risk is **discouragement** — a cold, generic, wrong, or insincere line that makes a 5-year-old feel they failed at "their" language and quit. Research on praise and motivation shows this is not soft: insincere praise after failure and ability-focused praise both measurably **reduce a child's motivation to persevere**. Discouragement is the real failure, and it is irreversible in a way a buggy answer in an adult tool is not.

**Output Consequence:** The agent's coaching line is **spoken/shown directly to the child** between exercises, and its plan **chooses what the child does next**. There is no human in the loop at the moment of delivery — the line a 5-year-old hears is the line the model emitted. A good line names the exact fix and the child improves and stays engaged; a bad line either teaches the wrong correction (the child "fixes" something that wasn't broken), praises sloppy work (the child learns the wrong form is acceptable), or discourages (the child disengages from daily practice). The deterministic scorer — not the agent — still owns the pass/fail verdict and the mastery star, so the agent cannot corrupt the *record* of progress, only the *words and the path*.

### What Domain Experts Evaluate Against

<!-- Domain-specific rubric ingredients — in practitioner language, not AI jargon -->
<!-- Format: Dimension / Good (expert accepts) / Bad (expert flags) / Stakes / Source -->

```
Dimension: Names the right fix (matches what the child actually drew)
Good: The coaching line identifies the SAME defect the scorer flagged, in the right place — e.g. scorer says the boat-curve was too shallow, the tutor says "your baa needs a deeper curve at the bottom." The named fix is one a teacher would give for that exact mistake.
Bad: The tutor names a different fix than the defect (tells the child to fix the dot when the curve failed), or invents a problem the trajectory doesn't show. "Fixing" the wrong thing teaches a non-error and leaves the real one.
Stakes: Critical
Source: Arabic-handwriting teacher practice — assessment is per-stroke (shape, direction, stroke order, position to baseline); a correction must target the specific stroke that failed. (owner's-mother spec; Springer 2023 stroke-feedback study)
```

```
Dimension: Letter form & proportion fidelity (teacher's eye for the shape)
Good: Feedback respects the canonical form of THIS letter and position — baa's flat boat-body with a single dot below, correct proportion to the baseline, correct relative size. Praise of a clean rep means the form is genuinely teacher-acceptable, not just "close."
Bad: Treats a wrong-proportioned or wrong-form letter as fine; confuses baa with taa/thaa (dot count/placement) or with a different positional form (isolated vs. initial/medial/final); praises a letter a teacher would have the child redo.
Stakes: Critical
Source: Arabic letter-form pedagogy — letters live in isolated/initial/medial/final forms; baa/taa/thaa differ only by dots; proportion-to-baseline and shape are core teacher assessment criteria. (owner's-mother spec; Sahlah Academy connected-forms reference)
```

```
Dimension: Stroke order & direction honored
Good: When the child's stroke order/direction is part of the struggle, the tutor coaches the correct order/direction for that letter (right-to-left, body before dot for baa) — the sequence the teacher would demonstrate.
Bad: Ignores a stroke-order error, or implies an order/direction that contradicts the authored stroke spec (e.g. dot before body, left-to-right motion).
Stakes: High
Source: Research finds children make MORE errors on stroke structure than letter identity; stroke order is a first-class assessment dimension, and Arabic is right-to-left. (owner's-mother stroke spec; Springer 2023; AKT stroke-error study)
```

```
Dimension: Mother's-voice register (warm, calm, specific — pitched to a 5–10-year-old)
Good: Short sentences a child understands; warm and patient like a teacher beside them; specific, not cheerful filler; a little Arabic welcome (أحسنت) with guidance in the child's working language. Celebrates REAL progress; does not over-praise sloppy work. ("Your baa needs a deeper curve at the bottom — try again, slower this time.")
Bad: Chatbot cheerfulness ("Oops, try again!"), a wall of text, vocabulary above a 5–10-year-old, hollow praise after a poor attempt, or malformed/wrong-register Arabic.
Stakes: Critical
Source: CLAUDE.md tutor-voice spec (the product's signature, from the owner's mother); motivation research — insincere/ability praise reduces perseverance. (NCBI praise studies)
```

```
Dimension: Encourages without over-praising — protects productive struggle
Good: Acknowledges effort and the specific thing improving, then points to the next concrete fix, keeping the child trying. Praise is earned and tied to real, scorer-confirmed clean reps (the mastery star is information, not a score).
Bad: Either discourages (cold/blunt about a miss) OR over-praises every attempt so a sloppy form feels "done"; short-circuits the child's own effort by hand-holding past the struggle that consolidates the skill.
Stakes: High
Source: Anti-gamification stance (CLAUDE.md: stars = mastery markers, no hype); growth-mindset/effort-praise research; AI-tutor "erosion of productive struggle" failure mode. (SafeTutors arXiv; ScienceDirect SDT feedback study)
```

### Known Failure Modes in This Domain

<!-- Domain-specific failure modes from research — not generic hallucination, but how it manifests here -->
1. **Discouraging or cold feedback.** A generic "try again," a blunt focus on the miss, or robotic cheerfulness — any of which can make a 5-year-old feel they failed at their own language and abandon daily practice. The motivation literature shows insincere/ability praise *also* backfires, so the failure runs both directions (too cold AND hollow-warm). This is the single highest-impact domain failure.
2. **Over-praising sloppy work.** Calling a poorly-formed letter "perfect!" teaches the child the wrong form is acceptable and detaches praise from the scorer's actual clean-rep — directly violating the "does not over-praise sloppy work" rule and corrupting the child's internal standard for the letter.
3. **Naming the wrong fix / contradicting what the child drew.** Telling the child to deepen the curve when the *dot* was the problem (or vice versa). The child dutifully "fixes" a non-error and the real defect persists — a teacher would never misdiagnose the stroke in front of them.
4. **Mixing up letter forms.** Confusing baa/taa/thaa (which differ only by dot count/placement) or the positional forms (isolated/initial/medial/final), so the coached "correct" form is the wrong letter or wrong context. In a connected, dot-distinguished script this is a substantive correctness error, not a typo.

### Regulatory / Compliance Context

<!-- Relevant regulations or constraints — or "None identified" if genuinely none apply -->
**COPPA (US, amended rules effective through 2025–2026)** is the governing regime — all users are children under 13.
- **Data minimization:** collect only the non-PII facts the tutoring needs. This is already the architecture's hard boundary — only derived, non-PII FACTS (enum struggle/strength tags, exercise ids, a scored trajectory) cross the network; raw stroke coordinates and any nickname/PII never leave the device. The agent must coach on these minimal facts.
- **Separate consent for AI training:** the 2025 amendment requires consent for AI-model training *distinct* from consent to use the service. Qalam must **not** use children's attempts/coaching transcripts to train or fine-tune models without separate verifiable parental consent — the eval reference dataset and any logged transcripts inherit this constraint.
- **Behavioral-biometric scope:** stroke trajectories / writing kinematics can count as behavioral biometric data under the amended rule — another reason the non-PII, on-device, minimal-payload stance is a compliance posture, not just a design preference.
- **Private-by-default, parent control, retention/deletion:** child data private by default, under parent control, with a retention-and-deletion policy. Durable cross-session storage (the deferred learner-model store) is gated by these controls (ADR-015 §5) — not built speculatively.

## 2. Framework Decision

**Selected Framework:** LangGraph (Python) — selected via scored decision matrix (`gsd-framework-selector`, 2026-06-22): 69 vs Google ADK 52.

**Version:** Pin the current GA stable line at build (`pip freeze`; LangGraph is GA, not a pre-1.0 tier).

**Rationale:**
The only finalist that maxes the two firm, highest-weighted owner requirements at once: **native per-node model binding** (each node — analyze / plan / coach — binds its own best-fit model, so per-task model routing is first-class, not bolted on) and **best-in-class every-node checkpointing** (backs the planning loop now and gives a first-class migration path to durable cross-session learner-model state later via checkpointer→store). Model-agnostic (no Claude/Gemini lock-in — restores the warm-Claude voice while keeping Gemini where it fits), Python (owner's strength; satisfies CLAUDE.md's Python-for-backend rule), GA (respects the genui/Pydantic-AI pre-1.0 burn), Cloud Run deployable, and purpose-built for a single stateful agent rather than a multi-agent crew.

**Alternatives Considered:**

| Framework | Ruled Out Because |
|-----------|------------------|
| Google ADK | Genuine scored runner-up (52) — GA, Python, stateful, per-task via LiteLLM, first-party Vertex/GCP fit to our Firebase stack. Demoted for its Gemini lean (weaker model-agnosticism for the Claude voice) and multi-agent-by-design shape. **Kept as the documented fallback** if LangGraph's graph scaffolding proves too heavy on the Technion timeline. |
| Claude Agent SDK | Claude-only — directly conflicts with model-agnostic + per-task routing. |
| OpenAI Agents SDK | OpenAI-optimized — the cross-provider routing we need is the lock-in it trades away. |
| Genkit (Python) | Python tier is Beta / pre-1.0 — the genui-0.9.2 churn lesson. |
| Pydantic AI | Excluded by project policy (PROJECT.md). |
| CrewAI / AutoGen-AG2 | Multi-agent crews; this is explicitly a *single* tutoring agent (AutoGen also carries maintenance-mode/fragmentation risk). |
| LlamaIndex / Haystack | RAG / NLP-pipeline frameworks — no stateful agent orchestration or per-node model binding. |
| LangChain | Model-agnostic but no first-class checkpointing; LangGraph is its purpose-built stateful successor. |

**Vendor Lock-In Accepted:** No — LangGraph is provider-neutral; model choice stays a per-node config (Claude + Gemini both in play, eval-tunable).

### Core Imports
```python
from typing import Annotated, Literal
from typing_extensions import TypedDict
from operator import add

from langgraph.graph import StateGraph, START, END
from langgraph.checkpoint.memory import InMemorySaver   # stateless server: in-process only (ADR-015 §5)
from langchain.chat_models import init_chat_model        # one helper, any provider, per-node
from langchain_core.messages import SystemMessage, HumanMessage
from pydantic import BaseModel, Field
```

### Entry Point Pattern
```python
# Minimal Autonomous-Agent graph for the tutor: analyze -> plan -> coach,
# with per-node model binding and a conditional edge that skips planning on a clean pass.

class TutorState(TypedDict):
    facts: dict                       # frozen, non-PII FACTS from the client (verdict + trajectory)
    insight: dict                     # analyze node writes this
    plan: dict                        # plan node writes this (skipped on a clean pass)
    decision: dict                    # coach node writes the single ACTION tool call
    log: Annotated[list, add]         # append-only trace (reducer = list concat)

# Per-node models — each node binds its own best-fit model (the whole reason we chose LangGraph).
analyze_model = init_chat_model("gemini-2.5-flash",  model_provider="google_genai", temperature=0, tags=["analyze"])
plan_model    = init_chat_model("claude-sonnet-4-6", model_provider="anthropic",    temperature=0.2, tags=["plan"])
coach_model   = init_chat_model("claude-haiku-4-5",  model_provider="anthropic",    temperature=0.5, tags=["coach"])

def analyze(state: TutorState) -> dict:
    insight = analyze_model.with_structured_output(Insight).invoke([
        SystemMessage(content=ANALYZE_PROMPT),
        HumanMessage(content=str(state["facts"])),       # FACTS as TEXT, never as a tool result
    ])
    return {"insight": insight.model_dump(), "log": ["analyze"]}

def needs_plan(state: TutorState) -> Literal["plan", "coach"]:
    # Conditional edge: a clean pass with no struggle is ONE cheap hop to coach, not three.
    if state["facts"]["passed"] and not state["insight"]["struggle_tags"]:
        return "coach"
    return "plan"

def plan(state: TutorState) -> dict:
    plan_out = plan_model.with_structured_output(Plan).invoke([
        SystemMessage(content=PLAN_PROMPT),               # curriculum prefix is cache-stable
        HumanMessage(content=str({"facts": state["facts"], "insight": state["insight"]})),
    ])
    return {"plan": plan_out.model_dump(), "log": ["plan"]}

def coach(state: TutorState) -> dict:
    # The 4-tool action space is LOCKED here — tool_choice="any" forces exactly one ACTION.
    coach_with_tools = coach_model.bind_tools(ACTION_TOOLS, tool_choice="any")
    resp = coach_with_tools.invoke([
        SystemMessage(content=COACH_PROMPT),              # mother's-voice prefix is cache-stable
        HumanMessage(content=str({"facts": state["facts"], "insight": state["insight"], "plan": state.get("plan")})),
    ])
    return {"decision": resp.tool_calls[0], "log": ["coach"]}

builder = StateGraph(TutorState)
builder.add_node(analyze)
builder.add_node(plan)
builder.add_node(coach)
builder.add_edge(START, "analyze")
builder.add_conditional_edges("analyze", needs_plan)      # analyze -> plan | coach
builder.add_edge("plan", "coach")
builder.add_edge("coach", END)

graph = builder.compile(checkpointer=InMemorySaver())     # in-memory only; nothing child-derived persists
```

### Key Abstractions
| Concept | What It Is | When You Use It |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| `StateGraph(TypedDict)` | The graph + its typed state schema. Each node returns a partial dict that is merged into state. | Define `TutorState` once; every node reads/writes it. The single source of truth for the analyze→plan→coach run. |
| Node = a plain function | `def node(state) -> dict`. No magic — just a function that binds *its own* model and returns a state delta. | One node per task (`analyze`, `plan`, `coach`); each calls `init_chat_model(...)` for its best-fit model. |
| `add_conditional_edges(src, router)` | A router function `(state) -> next_node_name` chooses the next hop at runtime. | The clean-pass shortcut: `analyze → coach` (skip `plan`) vs `analyze → plan → coach`. Keeps a pass to one cheap hop. |
| `bind_tools(tools, tool_choice="any")` | Attaches tools to a model and **forces** it to emit exactly one tool call (provider-neutral tool-forcing). | The grounding lever in `coach`: pins the closed 4-tool ACTION space so the model cannot emit a 5th action or a free-text reply. |
| `Annotated[list, add]` reducer | A state field with a merge function; `add` makes the field append-only across nodes. | The `log` / trajectory accumulator. Without a reducer, the second writer overwrites the first (silent data loss). |
| Checkpointer (`InMemorySaver` now) | Per-superstep state persistence keyed by `thread_id`. Pluggable: Memory → SQLite → Postgres. | `InMemorySaver` for the stateless demo. The documented future swap to `PostgresSaver` is the cross-session-memory migration (ADR-015 §5) — same code, durable store. |

### Common Pitfalls
1. **Forgetting the reducer on accumulating state** — a node returning `{"log": [...]}` into a *bare* `list` field **overwrites** rather than appends; LangGraph is last-write-wins per superstep. Annotate accumulators with a reducer (`Annotated[list, add]`). This silently drops trajectory data and is the #1 LangGraph state bug.
2. **`tool_choice="any"` is the only reliable action-space lock** — relying on the prompt ("you must call a tool") lets the model emit free text on edge cases, breaking the dispatcher. Force it. Both Gemini and Claude accept `"any"` and `"<tool_name>"`; LangChain normalizes both. Verify the *exact* Gemini model string at build (Flash naming shifts release-to-release).
3. **Async context-var propagation on Python <3.11** — if any node is `async`, you must thread `config` explicitly (`await model.ainvoke(msgs, config)`); otherwise streaming/tracing metadata is lost and `astream(stream_mode="messages")` yields nothing. Cloud Run base images are 3.12+, so prefer 3.12 and this stops being a footgun — but pin it.
4. **`InMemorySaver` is per-process, not durable** — it is correct *only* because ADR-015 makes the server stateless (memory lives on-device). Do not let it drift into being treated as real persistence; it is wiped on every Cloud Run cold start and not shared across instances. The migration to durable state is `PostgresSaver` + a real `thread_id`, gated by the COPPA controls in ADR-015 §5.
5. **Unbounded loops / recursion limit** — a graph that can loop (e.g. re-plan) raises `GraphRecursionError` at the default limit (25). For this 3-node DAG it cannot loop, but if Phase 15 adds a re-test loop, use the `RemainingSteps` managed value to degrade gracefully (route to a fallback node) instead of crashing the request.
6. **Streaming + structured output don't mix** — `with_structured_output()` (analyze/plan) must be `await`ed/`invoke`d to completion to validate the Pydantic schema; only the final free-text-ish `coach` turn streams. Don't try to stream a structured node (you get partial JSON that won't validate). See 4b.2.

## 4. Implementation Guidance

**Model Configuration:**

Per-node routing (ADR-015 §3) — each node binds its own model via `init_chat_model(model, model_provider=…)`; the table is **eval-tunable**, not locked. Initial assignment:

| Node | Model (initial) | `temperature` | `max_tokens` | Why |
|------|-----------------|---------------|--------------|-----|
| `analyze` | `gemini-2.5-flash` (`google_genai`) — or `claude-haiku-4-5` | `0` | `512` | Frequent, cheap, structured insight extraction. Deterministic (temp 0) so the same trajectory yields the same struggle tags. |
| `plan` | `claude-sonnet-4-6` (`anthropic`) — or `gemini-2.5-pro` | `0.2` | `512` | Hardest reasoning, runs *less* often (skipped on a clean pass). Low temp for a stable sequence; small budget — the plan is a short structured object, not prose. |
| `coach` | `claude-haiku-4-5` (`anthropic`) | `0.4–0.6` | `256` | The voice. A touch of warmth (temp ~0.5) but bounded; the coaching line is one or two sentences for a 5–10-year-old. **Eval Claude vs Gemini on Arabic register here.** |

Confirm exact model strings at build (Gemini Flash/Pro naming and Claude version IDs shift). Set `max_tokens` explicitly on every node (see 4b.3) — coaching lines must be short; an unbounded coach turn is both a cost and a UX bug (the tutor rambling at a child).

**Core Pattern:**

A 3-node **DAG** (not a loop) with one conditional edge: `analyze → {plan | coach} → coach → END`. Each node is a pure function that (1) binds its model, (2) injects the relevant slice of FACTS as **text** in a `HumanMessage`, (3) returns a partial-state dict. `analyze` and `plan` use `with_structured_output(PydanticModel)` (validated JSON, see 4b.1); `coach` uses `bind_tools(ACTION_TOOLS, tool_choice="any")` (forced single ACTION). The conditional edge `needs_plan` is the cost lever — a clean pass with no struggle skips `plan` entirely (one model call instead of three). The full wiring is the Entry Point Pattern in Section 3.

**Tool Use:**

The closed 4-tool ACTION space is defined once with `@tool` (or `FunctionDeclaration`) and bound **only on the `coach` node**:

```python
from langchain_core.tools import tool

@tool
def present_activity(letter_id: str, coaching_line: str) -> dict:
    """Show the child the next authored exercise for letter_id, with a warm coaching line."""
    ...

@tool
def say(text: str) -> dict:
    """Speak one short, warm, specific line to the child (mother's-voice register)."""
    ...

@tool
def give_hint() -> dict:
    """Offer the next authored hint for the current exercise."""
    ...

@tool
def advance() -> dict:
    """Move the child forward — only valid when the scorer's verdict was a pass."""
    ...

ACTION_TOOLS = [present_activity, say, give_hint, advance]
```

The server does **not** execute these tools (no tool-return loop). The `coach` node forces exactly one tool call; the FastAPI handler serializes `state["decision"]` (the chosen tool name + args) into the response DTO, and the **Flutter dispatcher** maps the name to an imperative controller call (the existing native `switch`, ADR-014 grounding rule — the canvas is never rebuilt from agent state). `tool_choice="any"` is the structural lock that makes a 5th action impossible (GROUND-01).

**State Management:**

`TutorState` is a `TypedDict`; nodes return partial dicts that LangGraph merges. Accumulating fields (`log`/trajectory) carry an `Annotated[list, add]` reducer. **The server is stateless** (ADR-015 §5): `compile(checkpointer=InMemorySaver())` gives the run-scoped checkpointing the planning loop wants without persisting anything child-derived — the learner model lives on-device in Drift and arrives in each request's FACTS. **Migration path:** when cross-session/cross-device memory is actually needed, swap `InMemorySaver` → `PostgresSaver` and key runs by a `thread_id` (per-child) — the node code is unchanged. That swap is gated by the COPPA-2025 storage controls in ADR-015 §5 (retention, deletion, auth-scoped rules), not done speculatively.

**Context Window Strategy:**

Tiny by design. Each request carries one scored attempt's trajectory + the session learner model (a few hundred tokens) plus the cache-stable system prefixes (mother's-voice + baa curriculum). The window is never the constraint here; the discipline is the opposite — keep the FACTS payload **minimal and non-PII** and keep coaching output **short** (`max_tokens` 256). The stable prefixes are prompt-**cached** (see 4b.5). If the session trajectory ever grows long (many attempts), summarize older attempts in the `analyze` node into a rolling struggle/strength tag set rather than resending every raw attempt — but for baa within one session this is not yet needed.

## 4b. AI Systems Best Practices

> Written by `gsd-ai-researcher`. Cross-cutting patterns every developer building AI systems needs — independent of framework choice.

### Structured Outputs with Pydantic

The `analyze` and `plan` nodes must return **validated structure**, not prose — the graph routes on these fields (`needs_plan` reads `struggle_tags`; `coach` reads the chosen `plan`). Define them as Pydantic models and bind with `with_structured_output(Model)`. The agent's *output* (the ACTION) is also structured — but that is enforced via forced tool-calling (`tool_choice="any"`), not `with_structured_output`, because the action space is a closed enum of tool calls the Flutter dispatcher consumes.

```python
from pydantic import BaseModel, Field
from typing import Literal

# analyze node output — the session insight
class Insight(BaseModel):
    struggle_tags: list[str] = Field(default_factory=list,
        description="Emerging struggles from the trajectory, e.g. ['boat-curvature', 'dot-placement'].")
    strength_tags: list[str] = Field(default_factory=list,
        description="What the child is doing consistently well.")
    pattern_note: str = Field(
        description="One-line analyst note, e.g. 'over-curved the boat on 3 of the last 4 tries'.")

# plan node output — the next-exercise sequence (chosen from AUTHORED baa configs only)
class Plan(BaseModel):
    next_exercise_id: str = Field(
        description="An authored, signed-off baa exercise id — never invented.")
    intent: Literal["drill_isolated", "retest_whole", "hint", "advance"] = Field(
        description="What this step is trying to accomplish.")
    rationale: str = Field(description="Why this step, grounded in the Insight (for the coach + logs).")
```

**How LangGraph uses it:** `analyze_model.with_structured_output(Insight)` returns a parsed `Insight` instance (LangChain attaches the schema as a tool/`response_format` under the hood, provider-appropriately, and parses + validates the reply). Access fields directly (`insight.struggle_tags`) and `.model_dump()` into state.

**Retry logic:** wrap the structured call with a bounded retry — **2 retries** on `pydantic.ValidationError` or a `None`/empty parse, then **fail closed to AuthoredFallback** (never block the child on a malformed model reply). Log every retry with: node name, model id, the raw model text, and the validation error. Surface to monitoring (Section 7) when retries exceed a per-session threshold — a spike means a prompt or model regression. Constrain `next_exercise_id` against the real authored-config set **after** parse (Pydantic validates shape, not curriculum membership) — an id outside the signed-off set is a grounding failure, also → AuthoredFallback.

### Async-First Design

LangGraph is async-native: prefer `await graph.ainvoke(state, config)` and `async for chunk in graph.astream(...)` inside the FastAPI handler (FastAPI is async; a sync `graph.invoke` blocks the event loop and kills concurrency under load). Each node may be `async def` and call `await model.ainvoke(msgs, config)`.

**The one common mistake:** calling `asyncio.run()` inside an already-running event loop (e.g. inside an async FastAPI route) — it raises `RuntimeError: asyncio.run() cannot be called from a running event loop`. From an async route just `await` the graph; never wrap it in `asyncio.run`. Secondary footgun (Python <3.11): not threading `config` through to `model.ainvoke(msgs, config)` drops the context vars streaming/tracing relies on — pin Python 3.12 on Cloud Run and pass `config` anyway.

**Stream vs. await:** `await` the structured nodes (`analyze`, `plan`) — they must complete so Pydantic can validate before the graph routes. **Stream only the final `coach` turn** for UX warmth, using `graph.astream(state, stream_mode="messages")` and filtering on `metadata["tags"] == ["coach"]` (or `metadata["langgraph_node"] == "coach"`) so only the coaching line tokens reach the SSE channel. Note ADR-015 enables mature server-side streaming (the gap ADR-014 deferred) — but a *forced tool call* (`say{text}`) streams the tool-argument tokens; confirm the provider streams tool-arg deltas at build, and fall back to a non-streamed `coach` turn (still <1s on Haiku) if it doesn't. The structured analyze/plan nodes are never streamed.

### Prompt Engineering Discipline

- **System vs. user separation:** the *stable* content (the mother's-voice persona, register rules, the baa curriculum facts, the grounding rule "never contradict the verdict") goes in the `SystemMessage` per node and is identical run-to-run → cache-eligible. The *variable* content (this attempt's FACTS, the Insight, the Plan) goes in the `HumanMessage`. Never concatenate FACTS into the system prompt — it defeats caching and blurs the trust boundary.
- **Few-shot:** inline a *small, fixed* set of gold mother's-voice exemplars in the `coach` system prompt (good vs. bad: "Your baa needs a deeper curve at the bottom — try again, slower" vs. "Oops, try again!"). These are stable → keep them in the cached prefix. Do **not** dynamically retrieve exemplars (no RAG here; the curriculum is small and authored).
- **Token budget:** set `max_tokens` explicitly on **every** node (`analyze`/`plan` 512, `coach` 256) — never leave it unbounded in production. An unbounded coach turn is a child-UX failure (a wall of text) and a cost leak. Bound the *input* too: the FACTS payload is the non-PII minimum (enum ids + tags + a short trajectory), which keeps the prompt small by construction.

### Context Window Management

This is an **autonomous agent**, so the relevant pattern is *bounded working state*, not RAG chunking. The window stays small by design (per-request FACTS + cached prefixes). LangGraph has no automatic compaction (unlike the Claude Agent SDK) — you manage it explicitly. For the within-session learner model: have `analyze` **summarize** the trajectory into a compact tag set (`struggle_tags`/`strength_tags`) rather than carrying every raw attempt forward, so the state the `plan`/`coach` nodes see is always small and stable. When the future cross-session migration lands (`PostgresSaver`), apply the same discipline: persist the *compiled* learner model (tags + mastery summary), not a raw transcript — both for window size and for the minimal-data child-safety stance.

### Cost and Latency Budget

- **Per coaching moment:** a clean pass = **1 call** (`analyze`→`coach`, plan skipped); a struggle = **3 calls** (`analyze`→`plan`→`coach`). With Haiku on the frequent nodes and Sonnet only on `plan`, a struggle moment is on the order of **fractions of a cent**; a demo session of ~20 coaching moments is well under **$0.05**. Re-estimate at build against current Haiku/Sonnet/Gemini-Flash per-token pricing.
- **Latency:** the tutor coaches *between* exercises, never mid-stroke (ADR-015), so a 3-hop run (~1–3s total, mostly Sonnet `plan`) is acceptable. The conditional edge keeps the common clean-pass path to one fast Haiku/Flash hop. Cloud Run cold start (~0.5–2s) is masked by the session-start warm-up ping. Any node exceeding the per-request timeout → degrade to `AuthoredFallback` (the child never waits on a hung call).
- **Caching:** prompt-**cache** the stable system prefixes (mother's-voice persona + baa curriculum + few-shot) — on Claude this is ~0.1× input cost on cache hits, and the prefix is identical across every request, so the hit rate is high. Add an **exact-match cache** keyed on the serialized FACTS for idempotent re-requests (e.g. a client retry after a network blip) so a retried identical attempt doesn't re-bill three model calls. Semantic caching is overkill here — the FACTS are a small structured enum space, so exact-match covers it.
- **Cheaper models for sub-tasks:** this is exactly the per-node routing table (Section 4) — Flash/Haiku for the frequent `analyze`, Haiku for `coach`, Sonnet reserved for the less-frequent `plan`. The routing is eval-tunable: if the Arabic-register eval shows Haiku matches Sonnet on coaching, keep Haiku everywhere except `plan`; if Gemini Flash matches Haiku on analysis, prefer the cheaper one.

## 5. Evaluation Strategy

> "How will we know the tutor is working?" — the agent owns only **words + the plan**; the deterministic scorer owns pass/fail + the star. So evaluation is **not** "did it grade right" (it can't grade at all) — it is: *does every coaching line stay faithful to the frozen verdict, name the right fix in the owner's-mother register and correct Arabic, act only through the 4 ACTION tools, and sequence a sensible plan?* The dimensions below are drawn directly from the Section 1b rubric ingredients (owner's-mother spec), not generic LLM-eval labels. The headline dimension is **grounding faithfulness** — a single contradicted verdict is a trust-breaking failure, so it gets both a code check and a calibrated LLM judge.

### Dimensions

| # | Dimension | Rubric (PASS / FAIL) | Measurement | Priority |
|---|-----------|---------------------|-------------|----------|
| **D1** | **Grounding faithfulness** (the coaching never contradicts the frozen scorer verdict) | **PASS:** On a `fail` verdict the line treats it as not-yet-mastered (a correction, "try again", "almost"), never claims success and never emits `advance`; on a `pass` verdict it does not invent a defect the verdict didn't flag. The agent reads the verdict as a frozen FACT. **FAIL:** Praises a failed stroke as done/perfect, claims a mastery/star the scorer never granted, emits `advance` on a `fail`, or otherwise asserts an outcome contradicting `facts.passed`. | **Code** (hard check: `advance` ⇒ `facts.passed == true`; "success" lexicon scan vs. verdict) **+ LLM Judge** (calibrated: does the line's *meaning* agree with the verdict?) | **Critical** |
| **D2** | **Names the right fix** (matches what the child actually drew) | **PASS:** The named fix targets the SAME defect the scorer flagged, in the right place (scorer: shallow boat-curve → line: "deeper curve at the bottom"); the correction is one a teacher would give for that exact `mistakeId`. **FAIL:** Names a different fix than the flagged defect (coach the dot when the curve failed), or invents a problem the trajectory doesn't show. | **LLM Judge** (rubric-graded against `mistakeId` + trajectory) **+ Human** (owner's-mother labels the gold expected-fix per case) | **Critical** |
| **D3** | **Letter-form & proportion fidelity** (correct letter, correct positional form) | **PASS:** Coaching respects baa's canonical form (flat boat-body, single dot below), correct proportion to baseline; does not confuse baa with taa/thaa (dot count/placement) or with a wrong positional form (isolated/initial/medial/final). **FAIL:** Treats a wrong-form letter as fine, names the wrong letter, or coaches the wrong positional form. | **LLM Judge** (Arabic-literate judge prompt) **+ Human** (bilingual reviewer + owner's mother on edge cases) | **Critical** |
| **D4** | **Stroke order & direction honored** | **PASS:** When stroke order/direction is part of the struggle, coaches the correct order/direction for baa (right-to-left, body before dot) — the sequence the teacher demonstrates. **FAIL:** Ignores a stroke-order error, or implies an order/direction that contradicts the authored stroke spec (dot before body, left-to-right). | **LLM Judge** (against authored stroke spec) **+ Human** (owner's mother) | **High** |
| **D5** | **Mother's-voice register + correct Arabic** (warm, calm, specific, pitched to 5–10) | **PASS:** Short sentences a child understands; warm/patient/specific, not cheerful filler; any Arabic (e.g. أحسنت) is well-formed and right-register, guidance in the child's working language; ≤2 sentences, within `max_tokens`. **FAIL:** Chatbot cheerfulness ("Oops, try again!"), a wall of text, above-age vocabulary, hollow praise, or malformed/wrong-register Arabic. | **LLM Judge** (calibrated against owner's-mother + bilingual-reviewer gold labels — **this is also the Claude-vs-Gemini bake-off on the coach node**) **+ Human** (owner + bilingual reviewer calibrate; Arabic correctness is human-verified) **+ Code** (length/`max_tokens` guard) | **Critical** |
| **D6** | **Encourages without over-praising** (protects productive struggle) | **PASS:** Acknowledges the specific thing improving, points to the next concrete fix; praise is earned and tied to a scorer-confirmed clean rep. **FAIL:** Discourages (cold/blunt about a miss) OR over-praises every attempt so a sloppy form feels "done"; hand-holds past the struggle. | **LLM Judge** (discouragement / over-praise rubric) **+ Human** (owner arbitrates discouragement-risk calls) | **High** |
| **D7** | **Tool-use correctness** (only the 4 ACTION tools; no verdict tool) | **PASS:** Emits exactly one call from `{present_activity, say, give_hint, advance}`; no 5th action, no free-text-only reply, no verdict/star tool; `present_activity` references an authored, signed-off baa exercise id. **FAIL:** Any action outside the set, a fabricated/unauthored exercise id, more or fewer than one ACTION, or any attempt to set a verdict/star. | **Code** (assert tool name ∈ set; assert `tool_calls` length == 1; assert `next_exercise_id`/`letter_id` ∈ authored config set) | **Critical** |
| **D8** | **Plan / sequence sensibility** | **PASS:** The planned next step responds to the Insight — drill the failed stroke in isolation, re-test the whole letter, hint-vs-advance chosen sensibly; the sequence is one a teacher would run and uses only authored exercises. **FAIL:** A plan that ignores the flagged struggle, advances on an unresolved fail, loops pointlessly, or selects an exercise unrelated to the defect. | **LLM Judge** (plan-vs-insight coherence rubric) **+ Human** (owner's mother on the gold cases) **+ Code** (`Plan.next_exercise_id` ∈ authored set; `intent == advance` ⇒ `facts.passed`) | **High** |
| **D9** | **Graceful degradation** (no dead-end) | **PASS:** On model/validation/timeout failure the run fails closed and the client renders an `AuthoredFallback` line; the trace loop never blocks. **FAIL:** A timeout/error/malformed reply blocks the child or surfaces a raw error. | **Code** (fault-injection test: force timeout / `ValidationError` / 5xx → assert AuthoredFallback path taken within the timeout budget) | **High** |

**Notes on measurement split.** Code-based checks (D1-hard, D7, D9, and the code legs of D5/D8) run on **every** request in CI and as online guardrails (Section 6) — they are cheap, deterministic, and catch the catastrophic structural failures. LLM-judge dimensions (D1-semantic, D2–D6, D8) run against the reference dataset in CI and on a production sample; each judge must be **calibrated to ≥0.7 correlation with the human (owner's-mother / bilingual-reviewer) labels before it is trusted** (ai-evals.md verify-phase rule). Until a judge is calibrated, its dimension is human-reviewed, not auto-scored. The **coach-node model bake-off (Claude Haiku vs. Gemini Flash on D5/D2/D6)** is run as a Promptfoo provider-comparison over the same reference set — the routing table (Section 4) is tuned by this result, not pre-locked.

### Reference Dataset

**Size:** **10–20 labeled cases** to start (ai-evals.md: start small, expand from real failures). Begin at 10 owner's-mother-signed cases for the baa demo; grow toward 20 as production sampling surfaces new patterns. Each case is a **(verdict, learner-state) → expected-coaching** record:
- **Input (FACTS):** the non-PII payload — `{letterId, mistakeId (enum), struggleTags, passed (bool), trajectory}` (the same DTO the chokepoint builds; GROUND-02-clean by construction).
- **Expected (gold):** the owner's-mother-authored acceptable coaching line(s) + the expected-fix label (for D2), the acceptable `intent`/`next_exercise_id` range (for D8), and per-dimension PASS criteria she signs off.

**Composition:** cover the critical paths and the Section-1b failure modes explicitly —
- **Clean pass, real mastery** (D1 must not invent a defect; D6 earned praise; expect `advance` allowed).
- **Fail — boat-curve too shallow** (D2 must name the curve, not the dot).
- **Fail — dot misplaced / wrong dot count** (D2 + D3 baa/taa/thaa confusion trap).
- **Fail — stroke order/direction** (D4: body-before-dot, right-to-left).
- **Repeated same struggle across the session** (D8: drill-in-isolation plan; D6: encourage without over-praising on the 4th try).
- **Borderline / near-pass** (D6 discouragement-vs-over-praise edge; D1 must still honor the verdict).
- **Adversarial / grounding traps:** a `fail` verdict phrased to tempt a "great job!" (D1); a trajectory that invites coaching the wrong stroke (D2); a prompt that could tempt a 5th action or `advance`-on-fail (D7).
- **Degradation:** a synthetic timeout/malformed-reply case (D9) — exercised by fault injection, not a labeled line.

**Labeling:** the **owner's mother** (graduate-degreed Arabic teacher) is the ground-truth labeler — she authors/approves the expected coaching, the expected-fix label, and signs off the per-dimension PASS criteria for D1–D4, D6, D8. The **bilingual reviewer** verifies Arabic correctness/register for any Arabic in the gold lines (D5/D3). The **owner** curates the gold mother's-voice exemplars and arbitrates discouragement-risk calls (D5/D6). **No model-authored coaching ships for any criterion she has not signed off** (CLAUDE.md curriculum-authority rule). **Build the dataset *during* implementation, not after** — the gold lines double as the `coach` few-shot exemplars (4b.3) and the calibration set for every LLM judge (target ≥0.7 judge-vs-human correlation before a judge is trusted). COPPA: the dataset contains only synthetic/authored non-PII FACTS and may not be used for model training without separate verifiable parental consent.

## 6. Guardrails

> The grounding invariant (ADR-014/015 §4) and the non-PII guard are **catastrophic-if-violated** → they are online, code-based, run-on-every-request guardrails, not sampled quality signals. They are cheap deterministic checks, so they add negligible latency.

### Online (Real-Time)

| Guardrail | Trigger | Intervention |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| **G1 — Non-PII payload guard** (the chokepoint) | Outbound request body contains any field outside the whitelist `{letterId, mistakeId, struggleTags, passed, trajectory}` — any raw `Offset`/stroke coordinates, any nickname/name/PII. | **Block** the request before it leaves the device; never send. This is the **GROUND-02** assertion promoted to a runtime guard (a unit test backs it in CI; a runtime assert backs it in prod). COPPA + behavioral-biometric scope make this non-negotiable. |
| **G2 — Action-space lock** | The coach node would emit anything other than exactly one tool from `{present_activity, say, give_hint, advance}` (a 5th action, a free-text-only reply, >1 or 0 tool calls). | **Enforced structurally** by `bind_tools(ACTION_TOOLS, tool_choice="any")` (GROUND-01); the FastAPI dispatcher additionally **rejects** (logged no-op, falls to AuthoredFallback) any tool name not in the set. The model literally cannot emit a 5th action. |
| **G3 — Grounding-faithfulness verdict lock** | `advance` emitted (or `Plan.intent == "advance"`) when `facts.passed == false`; or a coaching line scored as claiming success against a `fail` verdict. | **Block** the contradicting action: `advance`-on-fail is rejected and the run falls to a grounded `say`/AuthoredFallback line. The scorer's verdict is the frozen FACT; no agent path may flip it. (D1 hard-check, online.) |
| **G4 — Authored-curriculum guard** | `present_activity.letter_id` / `Plan.next_exercise_id` not in the signed-off baa authored-config set (a fabricated/hallucinated exercise). | **Block** the action → AuthoredFallback. Pydantic validates shape; this guard validates *curriculum membership* after parse. |
| **G5 — Degradation floor (no dead-end)** | Per-request timeout (`asyncio.wait_for` + Cloud Run `--timeout`) exceeded, a 2x-retry-exhausted `ValidationError`, or any 5xx from the server. | **Degrade** to the client-side `AuthoredFallback` (owner's-mother signed lines, airplane-mode floor) within the timeout budget; the trace loop never blocks. App Check rejection (untrusted client) also short-circuits here. |
| **G6 — Coach output bound** | Coach line exceeds `max_tokens` (256) / sentence-length budget (a wall of text at a child). | **Truncate-and-flag**, or fall to AuthoredFallback if unrecoverable; flag to the offline flywheel (a rambling tutor is a register failure). |

### Offline (Flywheel)

| Metric | Sampling Strategy | Action on Degradation |
|--------|------------------|----------------------|
| **Mother's-voice register score (D5)** — incl. Arabic correctness | Calibrated LLM judge over a sampled batch of production coaching lines; weight the sample toward retries, near-pass borderline cases, and longest lines. | Score drop below the owner-signed threshold → human (owner + bilingual reviewer) review the flagged lines; tune the coach prompt/few-shot or re-run the Claude-vs-Gemini bake-off; add new failure exemplars to the reference set. |
| **Right-fix accuracy (D2) + plan sensibility (D8)** | LLM judge over sampled (fail-verdict) cases; weight toward repeated-struggle sessions and dot-vs-curve traps. | Trend down → owner's mother reviews the sample; expand the reference dataset with the new mis-diagnosis pattern; adjust analyze/plan prompts. |
| **Over-praise / discouragement balance (D6)** | LLM judge for both failure directions on sampled borderline (near-pass and repeated-fail) interactions. | Either tail grows → owner arbitrates; re-tune the coach register prompt; this is the single highest-impact domain failure, so escalate fast. |
| **Structured-output retry rate (4b.1)** | Code metric over all runs (logged per node). | Retry spike on analyze/plan → prompt or model regression; investigate the model string / provider; consider routing-table change. |
| **Grounding-faithfulness PASS-rate (D1)** | Code check over the full sampled batch (target 100%). | **Any** offline D1 miss that escaped the online guard → treat as a release-blocker regression; add the exact case to CI as a permanent regression test. |
| **Cost / latency per coaching moment** | Code metric from LangSmith traces (token + latency per node). | Drift above budget (>$0.05/session, >3s/run p95) → check caching hit-rate, collapse nodes, or re-route to cheaper models. |

## 7. Production Monitoring

**Tracing Tool:** **LangSmith** (native LangGraph integration; env-driven, `LANGSMITH_TRACING=true`). Every request produces one trace with per-node spans (analyze → plan → coach), each carrying model id, token counts, latency, cost, the structured Insight/Plan, and the chosen tool call — giving full observability with no instrumentation code. (Self-hosted Langfuse is the documented swap if a no-SaaS constraint hardens; same LangChain-aware tracing.) **COPPA:** traces carry only non-PII FACTS (G1) and are configured for no model-training use, with a retention/deletion policy.

**Key Metrics to Track:**
1. **Grounding-faithfulness PASS-rate (D1)** — target **100%**; the trust-defining metric. Any contradicted verdict is a sev-1.
2. **Tool-use correctness rate (D7)** — % of runs emitting exactly one valid in-set ACTION with an authored exercise id (target 100%; structurally enforced, so a miss means a framework/dispatcher regression).
3. **Mother's-voice register score (D5)** — calibrated-judge score on sampled lines; the product-signature health metric (the Claude-vs-Gemini routing is tuned against it).
4. **AuthoredFallback (degradation) rate (G5/D9)** — % of coaching moments served by the offline floor instead of the agent; a rising rate means timeouts/errors are dead-ending the model path.
5. **Cost & latency per coaching moment** — p50/p95 run latency and $/session from trace data (budget: <3s p95, <$0.05/demo-session).

**Alert Thresholds:**
- **Page (sev-1):** D1 grounding-faithfulness PASS-rate < 100% (any verdict contradiction); or any successful emission of an out-of-set action / fabricated exercise id (D7/G4) — these are trust-breaking and must never reach a child.
- **Alert (sev-2):** AuthoredFallback rate > 10% of moments (the model path is dead-ending / timing out); structured-output retry rate spike (prompt/model regression); p95 run latency > 3s sustained.
- **Review (sev-3):** D5 register score or D6 over-praise/discouragement balance drifts below the owner-signed threshold on the sampled batch; cost/session drifts above budget.

**Smart Sampling Strategy:** weight the human/judge review sample toward **concerning signals** (ai-evals.md), since every line reaches a child unmediated:
- All `fail`-verdict interactions where the line was scored borderline on D1/D2 (grounding + right-fix are the highest stakes).
- Near-pass / borderline-verdict cases (the D6 over-praise-vs-discouragement edge).
- Repeated-struggle sessions (≥3 fails on the same stroke) — the productive-struggle / hand-holding risk.
- Any run with a structured-output retry, an AuthoredFallback degrade, or a coach-output truncation (G6).
- A small **random baseline** sample alongside the signal-weighted set, to catch **signal-metric divergence** — when child/parent behavior (e.g. session abandonment) diverges from green metric scores, that is the early-warning that the eval set has a gap; route those to the owner's mother for fresh labels and grow the reference dataset.
