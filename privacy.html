Qalam — Privacy Policy

Effective date: 19 July 2026 · App: Qalam (com.technion.qalam) · A student project of Technion course 236272

Qalam teaches children to write Arabic letters by hand. It is designed for children and used under a parent's or guardian's supervision. We collect the minimum data the app needs, keep it private by default, and never sell it or use it for advertising.

Children never sign in

A child never creates an account and never enters an email address or password. A parent signs in once (email/password or Google Sign-In) to set the app up; the child then uses the app under the parent's account and never has credentials of their own. The parent settings area is additionally protected by a parent-created PIN. The account collects the parent's email address only.

What we collect

  • Child profile: a nickname entered at setup and practice progress (which letters were practiced, repetitions, mastery). Stored on the device and in our cloud database (Google Firebase) under the anonymous or parent identity.
  • Parent account: the parent's email address, used only for sign-in and account management.
  • Handwriting: the child's strokes are recognized and scored on the device. Raw handwriting and drawings are never uploaded and never stored on our servers.
  • AI coaching summaries: to generate the tutor's feedback, the app sends a limited practice summary (letter, exercise type, and scoring outcome) to our tutor service on Google Cloud, processed with Google Vertex AI. This summary contains no names, no free text typed by the child, and no handwriting images — the allowed fields are a fixed technical whitelist verified by automated tests.

What we do NOT do

  • No advertising and no ad networks.
  • No sale or sharing of personal data with third parties for marketing.
  • No location collection, no contacts, no camera or microphone access.
  • No social features, no public profiles, no messaging.

Where data lives

Cloud data is stored with Google Firebase (Firestore) and processed on Google Cloud. Practice data is keyed to an anonymous identity unless a parent links an account. Access is restricted by security rules.

Data retention & deletion

Local data can be removed by clearing the app's storage or uninstalling the app. To request deletion of cloud data (the child profile, progress, or a parent account), email us at the address below and we will delete it.

Children's privacy

Qalam is directed at children and built to protect them: minimal data, private by default, under parent control. We do not knowingly collect more personal information than the app needs to function, and we never use children's data for advertising or profiling.
