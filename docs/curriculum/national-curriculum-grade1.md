> This is the **official, authoritative** competency curriculum for grade 1 (الصف الأول).
> It is a **language/reading** curriculum: it defines WHAT a first-grader must know and
> the ORDER competencies build in. It does **not** specify stroke mechanics (stroke order,
> per-letter formation mistakes) — that stays the owner-mother's domain. The two are
> complementary: the national curriculum grounds the *what & the order*; she owns the *how*.

## Adaptation framing (applies to all domains)

- **"تعلّم ذي معنى" (meaningful learning)** is the spine of the 2014 adaptation —
  three moves: مواءمة (fit content to available time), تقسيم (split into 70% essential
  / 30% enrichment-and-depth), دمج (integrate higher-order thinking + varied,
  alternative assessment — portfolio, etc., not just a test).
- **70 / 30 essential-vs-enrichment** is the official split. For Qalam this maps cleanly:
  the *essential core* = recognizing the letter (sound/name/shape), its **positional
  forms**, and **copy-writing**; the *enrichment* = richer word/sentence work, dictation
  from memory, vocabulary breadth.

## Grade-1 competencies relevant to Qalam (الأصوات اللغوية وتمثيلها الكتابي)

Verbatim-sourced, condensed:

1. **معرفة الحروف: أصواتها، أسمائها، أشكالها، وكتابتها في مواقعها المختلفة في الكلمة** —
   know each letter's sound, name, shape, and **writing it in its different positions in
   the word** (isolated / initial / medial / final). → *This is the curriculum mandate
   behind baa's `forms` section. Positional forms are a grade-1 requirement, not optional.*
2. **التمييز بين الحروف المتشابهة رسمًا والمختلفة نقطًا، مثل: ب، ت، ث** — discriminate
   letters identical in skeleton, differing only by **dots** (ba / ta / tha). → *Directly
   the baa family. Dot count/placement is a named grade-1 competency; baa should be taught
   in contrast with ta/tha, and dot errors are curriculum-central, not cosmetic.*
3. **التمييز الصوتي بين الحروف المتقاربة في اللفظ** (س/ص، د/ض) — auditory discrimination
   of phonetically-close letters.
4. الحركات القصيرة (الضمة/الكسرة/الفتحة) and حروف المد (ا/و/ي); السكون; exposure to
   الشدة، التنوين، الحروف الشمسية (the last three in **listening/speaking only** at grade 1).
5. **تحليل كلمات إلى مقاطع وفونيمات** + **تركيب/كتابة كلمات من مقاطع معطاة** — phonemic
   analysis then building/writing words from given syllables. Note: الوعي الصوتي is done
   **orally first — the child hears the sounds without seeing the written word.**
6. **قراءة كلمات مألوفة أو غير مألوفة، مفردة أو داخل نص، مشكولة تشكيلًا تامًا، بطلاقة (بدقة وسرعة)**
   — read fully-vowelized familiar/unfamiliar words with fluency (accuracy + speed).

### Spelling / handwriting (الإملاء، الترقيم، الخط — grade 1)

The curriculum names an explicit **difficulty ramp** for writing:

- **إملاء منقول** (copy what's in front of you) → **إملاء منظور** (look, then write what you
  saw) → **إملاء غير منظور** (write from memory / dictation). Easiest → hardest, in that order.
- خط النسخ (naskh) is taught in grades 1–2 **because it matches the textbook font**; if a
  child writes in رقعة (ruq'ah) it is **not counted as an error**.
- Punctuation at grade 1: only **النقطة** and **علامة الاستفهام**.
- Copy with clear letters and **spacing between words**.

### Morphology / vocabulary signals (الصرف، دلالة الألفاظ — grade 1)

- **نحو (syntax) is REMOVED in grades 1–2** — focus on meaningful sentences in
  listening/speaking, not formal grammar. (Keeps the cognitive load on letters + words.)
- Vocabulary is **من البيئة القريبة** (family, school, clothes) and includes
  **بدائل في اللغة المعيارية لكلمات محكية** — standard-vs-spoken alternatives. → *Direct
  relevance to Qalam's heritage learners; validates baa's near-environment word choices
  (باب = door, كتاب = book).*

## What this means for Qalam / Phase 15

**The national curriculum independently endorses baa's section order as a real
prerequisite chain**, and supplies the **difficulty lattice** the agent's "rails" need:

| baa section | National-curriculum competency it serves | Difficulty basis |
|---|---|---|
| meet / watchTrace | letter sound + name + shape (#1) | foundation |
| forms | the letter **in its positions** (#1) | builds on shape |
| words | phonemic analysis + build words from syllables (#5) | builds on forms |
| listenWrite | the **منقول → منظور → غير منظور** writing ramp | copy < look-write < dictation |
| mastery | fluent fully-vowelized reading (#6) | integrates all above |

Two concrete uses for the **selection-policy** decision:

1. **The "rails" become curriculum-grounded, not just membership.** Forward order is a
   prerequisite chain the *national curriculum* backs, so section-gated progression is
   pedagogically defensible to any reviewer — not just our preference.
2. **Backward remediation has an authoritative difficulty order to follow.** When a child
   fails a dictation (غير منظور), the curriculum-endorsed move is to drop to منظور, then
   منقول. The agent's remediation can be grounded in this lattice rather than guessing.
